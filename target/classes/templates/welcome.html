About Us

EGERTON DAIRY PLANT

It is situated close to the university’s Science Complex and provides value addition services to milk and milk products from the 20,000 acre Ng’ongogeri Farm, which houses more than 500 dairy cattle.The dairy production unit collects close to 3,000 litres of milk daily.

OUR PRODUCTS

Pasturized Milk

It is widely utilized by the population because of the 'safety' it provides and is said to minimize the risk of contracting milk-borne bacteriaand diseases.It is prepared by heating milk to a temperature higher than the boiling point and then rapidly cooling it.This eventually removes the bacteria and other harmful particles found in conventionally produced milk.Our pasturized milk will give a taste and experience you've never had before.

Yoghurt

Yoghurt has a higher nutritional value than fresh milk due to the live cultures which provide immune boosting to the digestive system. A part from boosting immunity, it is a rich source of protein, nutrients such as calcium, vitamin B2, vitamin B12, Potassium etc. Children and the elderly will benefit more from it due to their enormous calcium requirement. Calcium is also important in the prevention of bone loss in women and bone mass development in all ages.

Fresh Milk

Fresh milk is known for its nutritious value to health benefits .It contains vitamin C which help in the formation of strong bones and teeth.It is also good for babies for their development and growth.




DAIRY MANAGEMENT FACTORY

The Management

The factory is being managed by the General manager who is under the Dean of students in the faculty of Dairy Technology.The general manager is responsible for the management of the factory, he oversees the day to day activity of the the factory, then gives the report to the Dean. The Dean on the other hand checks the affairs of the students in relation to the factory and the general welfare of students taking the course.The secretary is responsible for assing apointment to the manager.

Factory Background

Egerton Dairy factory is located in Njoro Nakuru county, kenya.It is inside Egerton university. The factory was started in the year 2001 as a laboratory where Egerton students taking bachelor in Dairy and Food sciences would take their practical work and do their research work. This was primary reason why it was made.As the capacity grew , it was made to full factory for processing milk and supplying to nearby towns like njoro,Nakuru.
